Peach season doesn't last long, and if you've ever bitten into a peach that was picked at the exact right moment, you know why that matters. Sweet, dripping down your wrist, gone from your counter within a couple of days because everyone in the house keeps sneaking one more.

This list of peach desserts covers the classics you'd expect and a few that might not be on your radar yet. Some take twenty minutes. Others are worth setting aside a Sunday afternoon for. All of them make good use of peaches while they're actually good.

### **Before You Start**

A quick note before the list. Ripe peaches should give slightly when you press near the stem and smell sweet even through the skin. If yours are still firm, leave them on the counter for a day or two before baking with them. Underripe peaches turn out mealy and bland no matter how good the recipe is.

### **The Peach Desserts**

#### **1. Classic Peach Cobbler**

There's a reason cobbler shows up on every peach dessert list, and it's not just nostalgia. Sliced peaches get tossed with sugar, a little lemon juice, and a pinch of cinnamon, then topped with a biscuit-like batter that bakes up golden and slightly crisp on top while staying soft underneath.

The peaches release their juice as they bake, which pools around the topping and thickens into something close to syrup. Serve it warm, ideally with vanilla ice cream melting into the cracks.

#### **2. Peach Crisp**

Crisp and cobbler get lumped together often, but the topping is where they split. Crisp uses a streusel made from oats, flour, butter, and brown sugar, rubbed together until it clumps loosely.

That topping bakes up crunchy in a way cobbler's biscuit layer never quite does. It's also faster to throw together, since there's no dough to roll or cut in. If you want peach dessert with almost no prep time, this is usually the one to reach for.

#### **3. Grilled Peaches With Honey**

Not every peach dessert needs an oven, especially in the middle of summer when turning one on feels like a punishment. Halve and pit your peaches, brush the cut side with a little oil, and grill them cut-side down for a few minutes until you get real char marks.

Drizzle with honey and a little flaky salt once they're off the heat. The caramelization brings out a depth of sweetness raw peaches don't have on their own. A scoop of mascarpone or Greek yogurt on top rounds it out nicely.

#### **4. Peach Galette**

A galette is a pie that skips the pan entirely. Roll out pie dough, arrange sliced peaches in the center, and fold the edges up and over, leaving the middle exposed.

It's more forgiving than a traditional pie since there's no crimping or lattice work involved, and it looks intentionally rustic even when it's a little uneven. The exposed peaches caramelize slightly at the edges while the crust turns flaky and golden. This is a good option if you want something that looks impressive but doesn't demand pastry skills you don't have.

#### **5. Peach Ice Cream**

Homemade peach ice cream tastes almost nothing like the store-bought version, mostly because fresh peaches carry a brightness that gets lost in mass production. Puree ripe peaches with a touch of lemon juice, then fold that into a simple custard base before churning.

If you don't have an ice cream maker, a no-churn version works too, using whipped cream folded into sweetened condensed milk and peach puree, then frozen until firm. Either way, this is one of those peach desserts that's worth making while the fruit is at its peak, since the flavor genuinely depends on it.

#### **6. Peach Crumble Bars**

Crumble bars solve the portability problem that most peach desserts run into. A shortbread-like base gets par-baked, topped with a peach filling, then finished with more of that same crumbly mixture on top.

Once they've cooled and set, you can cut them into squares and eat them by hand, which makes them a better option for picnics or lunchboxes than a bowl of cobbler ever will be. They also hold up well in the fridge for a few days, which not every peach dessert can claim.

#### **7. Peach Shortcake**

Swap the strawberries in a classic shortcake for sliced peaches and you get something just as good, if not better. Warm, buttery biscuits split in half, filled with lightly sweetened whipped cream and juicy peach slices that have been sitting in a little sugar until they release their juice.

The contrast between the warm biscuit, cold cream, and soft fruit is what makes this one work. It's simple enough for a weeknight but feels special enough for company.

#### **8. Peach Clafoutis**

Clafoutis is a French dessert that sits somewhere between a custard and a pancake. Sliced peaches get arranged in a buttered dish, then covered with a simple batter of eggs, flour, sugar, and milk before baking until puffed and just set.

It comes out of the oven looking dramatic, tall and golden, before deflating slightly as it cools. That's normal, not a failure. Dust it with powdered sugar and serve it slightly warm for a dessert that feels more elegant than the short ingredient list suggests.

#### **9. Peach Sorbet**

For something lighter than ice cream, peach sorbet delivers all the flavor without the dairy. Puree ripe peaches with a simple syrup made from sugar and water, add a splash of lemon juice to balance the sweetness, and freeze in an ice cream maker.

Because there's no cream or eggs involved, the peach flavor comes through more directly than it does in a custard-based dessert. This is a good option for anyone dealing with dairy sensitivities, or just looking for something that tastes cleaner after a heavy meal.

#### **10. Peach Upside-Down Cake**

Borrowing the technique from pineapple upside-down cake, this version layers sliced peaches in a buttery brown sugar mixture at the bottom of the pan before pouring cake batter on top. Once baked and flipped out of the pan, the peaches end up glazed and caramelized on top of a moist, tender cake.

It looks far more impressive than the actual effort involved, which makes it a solid choice when you want a "wow" dessert without spending your whole afternoon in the kitchen.

#### **11. Peach and Almond Tart**

Almond and peach are a pairing that shows up again and again for good reason. This tart uses a base of almond cream, made from ground almonds, butter, sugar, and eggs, spread into a tart shell and topped with fanned peach slices before baking.

The almond cream puffs slightly and turns golden around the fruit, adding a nutty richness that plain peach desserts don't have. It slices cleanly, which makes it a good option if you're serving a crowd and want something that looks put together on a plate.

#### **12. No-Bake Peach Cheesecake**

When it's too hot to touch the oven, a no-bake cheesecake solves the problem entirely. A graham cracker crust gets pressed into a pan, topped with a whipped mixture of cream cheese, sugar, and a little vanilla, then finished with a layer of fresh peach slices or a quick peach compote.

It sets in the fridge instead of the oven, which means the only real waiting is for it to firm up. Light, cool, and genuinely easy, this is the peach dessert to make when the heat has you avoiding the kitchen entirely.

### **How to Pick the Right One**

If you're short on time, grilled peaches or the no-bake cheesecake are your fastest options. If you want something that travels well, the crumble bars hold up better than anything with a syrupy filling. And if you're cooking for a crowd and want a dessert that looks like it took more effort than it did, the upside-down cake or the galette are worth the slight extra step.

There's no wrong answer here. Ripe peaches are forgiving, and most of these recipes can handle small substitutions, whether that's swapping the topping on a crisp or using a different crust for a tart.

### **A Quick Note on Enjoying These**

These are desserts, and they're meant to be treated that way. None of them need to be "healthy" to be worth making, but a few small choices, like using a bit less sugar in the filling or serving smaller portions alongside fresh fruit, can make it easier to fit them into how you normally eat. If you're managing a specific health condition or dietary restriction, it's worth checking with your doctor or a registered dietitian about how sweets like these fit into your plan.

### **Conckusion**

Peach season doesn't give you much of a window, so it's worth making the most of it while the fruit is actually good. Whether you're after something classic like cobbler or want to try a clafoutis for the first time, these twelve peach desserts cover most of what you'd want to make this summer.

Pick two or three, grab a few extra peaches at the market, and let the season do most of the work for you.
