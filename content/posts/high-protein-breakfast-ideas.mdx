Nobody has an hour to cook breakfast on a Tuesday. These five high-protein breakfast ideas take less than 10 minutes each. Every one hits at least 25 grams of protein, no blender full of powders to clean afterward.

## The Setup: Why Protein at Breakfast Actually Matters

A high-protein breakfast keeps you fuller past 10am, which means less grazing before lunch even shows up. That's the whole pitch. No magic, just fewer trips back to the snack drawer.

## Five Breakfasts Worth Actually Making

### 1. Greek Yogurt Bowl With Peanut Butter and Berries

One cup of plain, full-fat Greek yogurt already has about 20 grams of protein before you add anything. Stir in a tablespoon of peanut butter and a handful of frozen berries, and you're at 28 grams in under three minutes.

Skip the flavored, sugar-loaded yogurt cups. Plain yogurt with real fruit beats them on both protein and sugar content.

### 2. Two-Egg Scramble With Cottage Cheese Folded In

Whisk two eggs with a quarter cup of cottage cheese before it hits the pan. The cottage cheese melts into the eggs and adds roughly 12 grams of protein without changing the texture much at all.

Total time on the stove: about four minutes on medium heat, stirred often so it doesn't overcook.

### 3. Overnight Oats With Protein Powder Stirred In

Combine half a cup of oats, a scoop of protein powder, and three-quarters cup of milk in a jar the night before. By morning, it's ready, and you've got roughly 30 grams of protein with zero effort at breakfast time.

Add cinnamon or a spoon of nut butter in the morning if you want more flavor without more prep.

### 4. Cottage Cheese Toast With Everything Bagel Seasoning

Toast a slice of dense whole grain bread. Top with half a cup of cottage cheese and a shake of everything bagel seasoning. It sounds strange until you try it, and then it becomes a weekly habit.

This one lands around 18 grams of protein and takes exactly as long as your toaster needs.

### 5. Protein Shake With a Banana, Blended or Shaken

A scoop of whey or a plant-based blend, a banana, and a cup of milk gets you to 30-plus grams of protein. It takes about as long as rinsing a shaker bottle.

Not glamorous. Also genuinely fast on the mornings when ten minutes is optimistic.

## What If You Don't Like Any of These?

Swap the protein source, not the format. Silken tofu works in place of cottage cheese for a similar texture. Canned tuna on toast, unusual as it sounds, hits a similar number for savory mornings.

The format matters less than the protein number. Find the version that you'll actually make again on a Wednesday, not just once for the novelty.
